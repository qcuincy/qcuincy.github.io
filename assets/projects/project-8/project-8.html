Project 8

Sonar-Enhanced ASV for Freshwater Monitoring (Concept)

Overview:

  • Developed a business plan for an innovative Autonomous Surface Vehicle (ASV) designed for comprehensive freshwater monitoring, inspired by bio-inspired artificial intelligence principles.

Key Contributions:

  • Designed a sonar-enhanced ASV capable of detecting various types of sediment and pollutants in freshwater bodies
  • Implemented a ZigBee mesh network for uninterrupted communication and data transmission
  • Created a sustainable and eco-friendly monitoring solution to address critical environmental challenges
  • Developed a detailed business model and growth strategy for market implementation

Technical Implementations:

  • Advanced sensors including 3D sonar, pH probe, and Acoustic Doppler Current Profiler
  • Autonomous navigation systems with GPS, sonar sensors, and IMU
  • Data processing and analysis pipeline for real-time monitoring
  • Nature-inspired, 3D-printed waterproof shell for the ASV

Research Implications:

  • Addressed the limitations of current freshwater monitoring methods (manual sampling, fixed stations, remote sensing)
  • Proposed a solution to combat increasing flood damage and associated economic costs
  • Contributed to the growing global market for environmental monitoring technologies

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Bio-inspired design principles
  • Autonomous systems development
  • Environmental sensing and data analysis
  • Business planning and financial projections
  • Risk analysis and mitigation strategies
  • Sustainable technology development

Future Directions:

  • Prototype development and testing
  • Regulatory compliance and certifications
  • Pilot projects and proof of concept implementations
  • Expansion from local to international markets
